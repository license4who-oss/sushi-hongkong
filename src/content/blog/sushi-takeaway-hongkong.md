Hong Kong's busy lifestyle means sushi takeaway is a lifestyle staple. From department store counters to dedicated takeout specialists, quality options abound.

## Department Store Counters

**IFC Mall, Mitsukoshi**
- Fresh daily, high quality
- Price: HK$200-400 for 8-10 pieces
- Specialties: Chirashi boxes, nigiri sets
- Hours: 10am-8pm

**Sogo Causeway Bay**
- Wide variety, consistent quality
- Bento box sets popular
- Price: HK$150-350
- Location convenience: Tourist-friendly

**Cityplaza, Tai Koo**
- Less crowded than downtown
- Premium selections available
- Price: HK$250-500 for chef-special sets
- Good for lunch orders

## Standalone Takeaway Shops

**Genki Sushi To-Go**
- Multiple locations across Hong Kong
- Affordable: HK$80-200 for good portions
- Quality consistent, grab-and-go
- Popular for office workers

**Sushiro Takeout Window**
- Separate counter for takeaway
- Same quality as dine-in
- 10-15 min wait during lunch rush
- Best value option

## Delivery Services

**Foodpanda, Deliveroo**
- Most kaiten chains available
- 30-45 min delivery
- Add HK$20-40 delivery fee
- Quality: Good, not perfect (rice can harden)

**Restaurant Direct**
- Some high-end spots offer delivery
- Premium pricing (HK$30-50 upcharge)
- Better for cooked items than fresh sashimi
- Minimum order HK$300+

## Takeaway Storage Tips

**Optimal Freshness**
- Eat within 2 hours of purchase
- Keep in cool bag with ice pack
- Store in fridge if delayed (max 6 hours)
- Don't stack takeout boxes
- Remove from bag immediately to prevent condensation

**Best Takeaway Items**
✓ Cooked items (eel, shrimp tempura)
✓ Rolls with mayo/cooked elements
✓ Sashimi in vinegar (kohada, mackerel)
✓ Chirashi bowls (rice holds moisture)

✗ Delicate nigiri (gets soggy)
✗ Uni, scallop (texture compromised)
✗ Premium fresh fish (wasted potential)

## Pro Tips

- Order at non-peak hours for fresher selections
- Specify "as fresh as possible" when ordering
- Ask for separate ginger, wasabi packets
- Request rice temperature preference upfront
- Takeout sets usually 20% cheaper than dine-in equivalents

Perfect for office lunches, picnics, or quick dinners. Quality remains respectable for convenient, affordable sushi.
