Hong Kong's omakase scene rivals Tokyo, with world-class chefs crafting intimate 15-20 piece experiences using premium fish sourced directly from Japan.

## Michelin-Starred Omakase

**Sushi Shikon** (3 Michelin Stars) stands as Hong Kong's premier omakase destination. Chef Shinobu Imaasa trains only two apprentices at a time, maintaining exceptional quality. Expect HK$3,500+ per person.

**Sushi Saito** offers refined Edomae techniques with seasonal specialties. Booking requires advance reservation through your hotel concierge.

## Mid-Range Omakase (HK$800-1,500)

These restaurants deliver exceptional value without the premium pricing:

- **Sushi Tokami** — Consistent quality, friendly atmosphere
- **Midori Sushi** — Fresh daily imports from Tsukiji
- **Aburiya Omotesando** — Hybrid omakase + grilled options

## What to Expect

A proper omakase experience follows this flow:
1. **Seating** at the counter (most intimate)
2. **Light appetizers** — Miso soup, edamame
3. **Progressive nigiri** — 15-25 pieces, chef-selected
4. **Finale** — Hand roll or tamago (egg)
5. **Dessert** — Green tea or mango

Total time: 90-120 minutes. Trust the chef's pacing and selections.

## Booking Tips

- Reserve 2-4 weeks in advance
- Mention dietary restrictions upfront
- Arrive 10 minutes early
- Cash preferred (some accept cards with surcharge)
- Photography usually discouraged
