Understanding sushi etiquette ensures a respectful dining experience and shows appreciation for the chef's craft. Hong Kong's sushi culture values tradition and precision.

## Before You Eat

**Reservation Protocol**
- Book 1-4 weeks ahead for omakase
- Arrive 10 minutes early
- Mention dietary restrictions by phone, not in person
- Casual kaiten: Walk-ins welcome, expect short wait

**Seating Etiquette**
- Counter seating is most intimate
- Sit where directed, don't move seats
- Keep purses/bags off counter
- Elbows off table while eating

## How to Eat Sushi

**The Soy Sauce Rule** (MOST IMPORTANT)
- Dip fish-side down, NOT rice-side
- Rice absorbs too much liquid and falls apart
- Quick dip only — don't soak
- Light touch is sophisticated

**Chopsticks vs. Hands**
- Both acceptable in Hong Kong
- Many prefer hands for better control
- Use chopsticks for communal dishes (edamame, miso soup)
- Leave chopsticks parallel on plate when finished (not crossed)

**Eating Order**
1. Light/white fish first
2. Medium flavor fish next
3. Rich/fatty toro last
4. Cooked items toward end
5. Egg (tamago) as finale

## What NOT to Do

❌ Mix wasabi directly into soy sauce
❌ Tear nigiri in half
❌ Pick up sashimi with chopsticks aggressively
❌ Leave rice on plate (wasteful)
❌ Apply extra wasabi without asking chef
❌ Photograph dishes without permission
❌ Rush the experience
❌ Request temperature changes for fish

## Compliments & Gratitude

**Express Appreciation**
- "Oishii desu!" (It's delicious!) to the chef
- Nod of respect when served each piece
- "Gochisousama deshita" (Thank you for the meal) when leaving
- Tip 10-15% at casual spots; omakase included in bill

## At High-End Omakase

- Trust the chef's selections completely
- Don't ask for substitutions mid-meal
- Timing between pieces is intentional
- Silence during eating is respected
- Eye contact with chef shows engagement
- Thank chef personally before leaving

Proper etiquette isn't about rigid rules — it's showing respect for the chef's skill and the dining experience they've crafted for you.
