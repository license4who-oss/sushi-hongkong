Kaiten sushi (conveyor belt restaurants) offer Hong Kong's most accessible entry point to quality Japanese sushi culture, starting from just HK$12 per plate.

## Top Kaiten Chains

### Sushiro
- **Price**: HK$12-45 per plate
- **Locations**: Causeway Bay, Mong Kok, Kowloon Bay
- **Signature**: Chu-toro, scallop, sea urchin
- **Ordering**: Tablet system, tap to add dishes
- **Speed**: Fresh plates every 60 seconds

### Genki Sushi
- **Price**: HK$15-50 per plate
- **Locations**: Central, Tsim Sha Tsui, Quarry Bay
- **Specialty**: Salmon varieties, cooked items
- **System**: Automated track delivery
- **Vibe**: Family-friendly, casual

### Itamae Sushi
- **Price**: HK$14-48 per plate
- **Locations**: Sheung Wan, Mong Kok
- **Best for**: Consistency, premium selections
- **Ordering**: Direct from chef or tablet
- **Unique**: Live cooking counter visible

## Kaiten Etiquette

1. **Ordering** — Grab from conveyor or tap tablet
2. **Stacking** — Stack empty plates; server counts at end
3. **Color coding** — Plate color = price tier
4. **Pace** — Eat at your own speed, no time pressure
5. **Condiments** — Soy sauce, wasabi, ginger provided

## Pro Tips

- Go during off-peak hours (2-5pm) for fresher selections
- Check color code legend before ordering
- Premium plates (gold rim) worth the extra cost
- Cooked items (tempura, eel) rotate less frequently
- Average spend: HK$150-300 per person

Perfect for lunch breaks, casual dates, or family outings without the formal omakase commitment.
